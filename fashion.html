Fashion

A journey through my years as a fashion designer working in New York City.

2014 - 2019

Art and fashion had been an integral part of who I am as a designer today. I studied fine arts in high school and fashion design in college, and then worked for five years as a fashion designer at Club Monaco and Kate Spade.

There are many precious assets I’ve accrued from my five plus years of working in the fashion industry. It has taught me to iterate fast and think creatively, solve problems, craft stories, and look for connections in places where they are seemingly unconnected. This unique experience had greatly impacted me in how I think and approach problems as a designer.

I begin my process with research.

I would go to the library to study different subjects, such as art, photography, or history. I also went through archived collections of old magazines and designer look books to explore ideas and concepts.

With the research ideas on the board, I analyze those images and map out their relationships and themes.

Maybe it’s an aesthetic relationship: how the shapes relate, such as the cream mockup with the black piping that visually relates to the yellow vintage skirt, or a concept of how the 70’s tropical Caribbean is being carried through vibrant prints and colors. All these elements work together to craft into a story of travel, escape, and nostalgia.

Next, based on the ideas and rough mock ups I’ve made, I will sketch up versions of an idea and present them to design director and merchandising. The sketches are usually based off of mock ups I’ve made. I consider them equivalent to high fidelity prototypes in UX design. Then, working with technical design team, we send out detailed tech packs to factory to create the first samples.

When we receive the samples back from factory, we will go through rounds of fitting, usually three or more, and then the garments are ready to hit the store!

Fashion design, like all areas of design is a creative process used to create products that bring joy and delight to peoples’ lives.

As I reflect on these years as I work on this page for my website, they bring back memories of my 20-something year old self. Those were experiences I would never forget. They were great :).
