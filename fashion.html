Overview

I worked at Club Monaco as Associate designer for Tops and Skirts for four years. While I was there, I designed and developed all seasonal styles for the two categories. It was a creative process conducted through research, design iteration, and prototyping.

Time | Jan '13 - Dec '17
Role | Research, Design, Prototyping

Research

I begin my design process with research. I would go to the library to study different subjects, such as art, photography, or history. I also went through archived collections of old magazines and designer look books to explore ideas and concepts.

…then I start to play with those ideas on the dress form, and using textured fabrics
with novelty trims I would experiment and create interesting shapes.

Research is my favorite part of the design process.
I am free to explore, let my creative juices flow, and experiment with all kinds of ideas.

From my research, I would analyze those images on the board and map out their relationships and themes.
Maybe it’s an aesthetic relationship: how the shapes relate, such as the mockup with the piping that relates to a vintage skirt, or how a theme of 70’s tropical Caribbean is carried through vibrant prints and colors. All the elements are woven together into a story of travel, romanticism, and escape.

Sketching & Prototyping

Next, based on the ideas and rough mock ups I’ve made, I will sketch versions of an idea and present them to design director and merchandising. The sketches are usually based off of mock ups I’ve made. I consider them equivalent to high fidelity prototypes in UX design. Then, working with technical design team, we send out detailed tech packs to factory to create samples.

Final Products

Thoughts

Fashion design, like all types of design is a creative process used to create products that bring joy and beauty to peoples’ lives. There are many precious assets I’ve accrued from my five plus years of working in the fashion industry. It has taught me to iterate fast and think creatively, solve problems, craft stories, and look for connections in places where they are seemingly unconnected. This unique experience has immensely attributed to how I think and approach problems creatively as a designer.
