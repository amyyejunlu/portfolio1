Ideation Project for Parent-Child Contact in Work Separated Families

Time | March 2018
Role | Ideation (Adobe Illustrator)
Link | Ideation Project Report
Course | User Research & Design | User Interface Specialization (University of Minnesota)

Overview

This project was part of the User Research & Design course from the User Interface Design Specialization (University of Minnesota) from Coursera. The assignment required ideating 100 ideas to facilitate parent-child contact in work-separated families. A research paper on this topic was provided as basis for ideation. From the 100 ideas, I selected top 5 solutions and sketched them using Adobe Illustrator.

Project Introduction

"In work separated families, children and parents have developed strategies to manage being apart. The remote parent uses synchronous and spontaneous asynchronous communication to maintain a constant presence in the life of the child, including video chat, phone call, email, and mail. The collocated adult manages those interactions between children and the remote parent. Children, on the other hand, tend to focus on other activities and sources of support, such as the collocated adult, and the eventual reunion."

Opportunities:

  1. Designing for synchronous communication with multiple children

  2. Designing for direct asynchronous communication between the parent and child

  3. Designing for infrastructure-poor environments
