I'm Amy Lu.

Currently studying Master of Human-Computer Interaction at Carnegie Mellon University.

I graduated from Parsons School of Design in 2012, and worked as a fashion designer for five+ years. While working as a fashion designer, I’d always wondered how I can make a greater impact on people and the world.

In 2017, I traveled to Taiwan on my first mission’s trip. Serving the underprivileged population opened up my eyes to see the needs people faced, and how I could better use my skills and talents to help them. After I came back from the trip, I began taking courses on user experience design on Coursera.

Since then, it had been an incredible eye-opening journey. I felt great joy and satisfaction every time I was able to solve a problem and uncover new insights through human-centered design methodolgies. Design at its core is finding creative solutions and the constant drive to do better. My curiosity and drive to design meaningful solutions propels me to constantly explore and examine new ideas and outcomes.

I would love to hear from you! Connect with me on Linkedin or via email: amyyejun@gmail.com. ☺
