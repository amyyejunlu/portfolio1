Spotify Redesign

A redesign of Spotify’s mobile user interface interactions based on research results

Time | July - Aug 2018
Role | User Research, Design (Sketch), Prototype (Protopie)

The Challenge

Music is an essential part of my life. I listen to music with everything I do; while working, cooking, cleaning the house, walking the dog, or even waking up in the morning. I’ve been a Spotify premium user for over 2 years. Although Spotify has done a decent job in meeting my needs and recommending songs I like, there are still frustrating moments in the process that prevent it from delivering an ideal experience.

Interview

I interviewed 5 premium users from the age of 14 to 30, to better understand their experiences and pain points using Spotify. The interviews were conducted informally via contextual inquiries.

I asked them if they use the other tabs of the app, such as “Home”, “Browse”, and “Radio,” the consensus was no. Users found that those tabs offered nothing they liked and “they didn’t like to listen to the stuff suggested there”. Users spend the bulk of their time in “Your Library” listening to playlists they’ve created.

Information Architecture

To better understand how content is organized and structured, I created information architecture based on Spotify’s current design.

I found that there was a lot of repeated content. “Daily Mix”, “Discover Weekly”, “New Releases”, “Podcasts”, “Radio”, and “Stations” are repeated across four sections in the IA. Because users can find the same content in all 4 sections, they found it visually repetitive to look at the same redundant content, and would therefore disregard a section in its entirety.

The second issue I found was how irrelevant the recommended playlists are and how little information they give in helping users understand those playlists are customized for them. In “Home” tab, users see “mood booster”, “young & free”, podcasts, and Drake’s new album. If the user doesn’t like hip-hop, then Drake’s new album holds little value to him. Can we give users the choice to personalize and follow playlists they like?

User Journey

I asked users to walk me through their user journey. Four out of five participants had the same user journey: they would scroll to “Recently Played” and shuffle play a playlist.


Main Issues:



  1. Unused Functions

      2. Across participants I found that “Home”, “Browse”, and “Radio” are not used. Instead, users go straight to “Your Library” to listen to songs they like. They also use “Search” when they are looking for a particular song to listen to.

  2. Similar Names

      3. Radio vs. Stations
      Save vs. Downloaded
      Go to radio vs. Play Radio

      “Radio” and “Stations” are confusing copy that mean the same thing. It’s baffling trying to differentiate between their functionalities. Under “Radio”, there are “Create a station” and “Genre Stations”, but there are also “Genre Radio” and “Artist Radio”. “Stations” is also one of the options under “Your Library".

  3. Interaction Flaws

      4. Since users interact with playlists the most on Spotify, they pointed out that “Recently Played” playlist had too much stuff, and was cumbersome in finding the songs they are looking for.

Redesigned Information Architecture

I removed any repetitive content that users are seeing across sections and reorganized them under 3 main categories: “My Library”, “What’s New”and “Search”.

“Recently Played” is set to default and expanded when users launch the app. To access the other sections, users would click the titles to expand.

One user shared that the radio playlist she listens to was a version of her created playlist. So I placed “Radio” under “My Library” for intuitive navigation.

I placed suggested content only under “What’s New”. Users can choose to follow playlists they are interested in such as: “Discover Weekly”, “Release Radar”, or “Time Capsule”, and add them to the navigation bar under “What’s New”.

Redesigned User Journey

Instead of clicking through a series of navigations to access the playlist the user use the most, I made "Recently Played" the default view and automatically expanded when user launches the app. This makes it easier for the user to navigate quickly to find the playlist they want to listen to.


Call to Action:



  1. Redistribute and reorganize information in Information Architecture

  2. Personalize recommendations

  3. Redesign interactions for quick access

  4. Streamline copy
